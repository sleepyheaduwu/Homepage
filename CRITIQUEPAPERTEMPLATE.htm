Grit: The Strongest Predictor of Success

Speaker:

Angela Duckworth

Summary of the Video:

The speech was about Angela’s experiences when she was a teacher and a psychologist. She conducted studies wherein she asked leaders in business, art, sports, medicine, military, and schools about what sets the top performers in their field apart from the rest. She came up with a conclusion that grit is what makes them successful/achievers because they would have passion and perseverance for what they do.

Introduction:

The speech started talking about Angela Duckworth’s experience when she was a teacher. While working she noticed IQ wasn’t the only difference of her best and worst students.  After several years of her teaching, she came into a conclusion that a motivational learning is what the students need.

Body:

She left her teaching career and when into Psychology. She would conduct studies wherein she would ask and predict who would be successful in different context. She came with a conclusion that 1 characteristic emerged as a predictor of success, and it was Grit. Grit is passion and perseverance for very long term goals. She is saying that to achieve success you need to have grit where you work hard everyday to achieve your goals/future. She said that the best idea of building grit in kids is the “growth mindset.” It was an idea developed by Carol Dweck, and it is the belief that the ability to learn is not fixed and it can change with your effort.

 

 

Conclusion: The speech ended with her statement that said we need to take our best ideas, our intuitions and we need to test them. We need to measure whether we’ve been successful and we have to be willing to fail to start over again with lessons learned. In short she said we need to be gritty if we want to turn our future into reality and if we want our kids to be successful.
